Class 6 Science - What Do We Eat?
20 Fill in the Blanks
================================================================================

================================================================================
EASY LEVEL (7 questions)
================================================================================

1. You may have noticed that there is a ________ in the food eaten by you and your friends.
   Answer: variety

2. The major nutrients in our food are carbohydrates, fats, proteins, vitamins, and ________.
   Answer: minerals

3. Carbohydrates and fats are primary ________ sources.
   Answer: energy

4. Protein-rich foods are called ________-building foods because they help in growth and repair of our body.
   Answer: body

5. A diet that has all essential nutrients, roughage, and water in the right amount for proper growth and development of the body is known as a ________ diet.
   Answer: balanced

6. Millets are known as ________ as they provide most of the nutrients required for the normal functioning of our bodies.
   Answer: nutri-cereals

7. The entire distance travelled by a food item, from the place of its production to the consumer, is called ________.
   Answer: food miles

================================================================================
MEDIUM LEVEL (7 questions)
================================================================================

1. The traditional food of any state is usually based on the ________ grown in that state.
   Answer: crops

2. ________ and fats are primary energy sources for our body.
   Answer: Carbohydrates

3. Protein-rich foods are also known as ________ foods because they help in growth and repair of our body.
   Answer: body-building

4. A diet that has all essential nutrients, roughage, and water in the right amount for proper growth and development of the body is known as a ________.
   Answer: balanced diet

5. Jowar, Bajra, and Ragi are examples of highly nutritious grains also known as ________.
   Answer: millets

6. The entire distance travelled by a food item, from its producer to the consumer, is known as its ________.
   Answer: food miles

7. Technological development, improved transportation, and better communication are factors that have caused changes in ________ practices over time.
   Answer: culinary

================================================================================
HARD LEVEL (6 questions)
================================================================================

1. The traditional food of any state is usually based on the ________ grown in that state, reflecting India's diverse soil and climate types.
   Answer: crops

2. While carbohydrates and fats are primary energy sources, ________ are specifically known as body-building nutrients, crucial for the growth and repair of our body.
   Answer: proteins

3. Minimising ________ is important because it helps to cut down the cost and pollution during transport, supports local farmers, and keeps our food fresh and healthy.
   Answer: food miles

4. The significant difference between traditional and modern culinary practices, such as using a *chulha* versus a gas stove, is largely due to factors like technological development, improved transportation, and ________.
   Answer: better communication

5. Junk foods are considered unhealthy as they contain high levels of sugar and fats but are notably low in protein, minerals, vitamins, and ________.
   Answer: dietary fibres

6. Millets are known as ________ because they provide most of the nutrients required for the normal functioning of our bodies and can be easily cultivated in different climatic conditions.
   Answer: nutri-cereals
